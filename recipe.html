Kothimbir vadi
Kothimbir vadi
Kothimbir vadi is a delicious savory crisp snack from the Maharashtrian cuisine made with gram flour (besan), coriander leaves, peanut, sesame seeds and spices. These savory, herby and flavorful bites can be pan-fried or deep-fried. They are a popular tea-time fritter snack loaded with coriander leaves (cilantro)
Prep Time
30min
Cook Time
15min
Serving
6 servings
Instructions:

1) firstly, in a large mixing bowl take 2 cups of chopped coriander leaves.
2) also add 1 cup of besan.
3) additionally add 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste, 1 green chilli, ½ tsp red chilli
powder,½ tsp turmeric, ½ tsp coriander powder, ½ tsp cumin powder, ¼ tsp garam masala
powder,
1 tsp sesame seeds, salt and 1 tsp lemon juice.
4) combine well making sure all the spices are mixed well.
5) also squeeze and mix making sure the coriander leaves are squeezed well.
6) further add water as required and begin to knead.
7) knead to soft dough.
8) shape the dough to cylindrical shape greasing hand with oil.
9) steam the dough for 15-20 minutes.
10) cool the dough completely and cut them into thick slices.
11) further, deep fry or shallow fry in hot oil.
12) stir occasionally till the wadi turns crisp and golden brown.
13) finally, serve kothimbir vadi garnished with coriander leaves along with cutting chai.

Ingredients

2 cups coriander leaves

1 cup besan / gram flour / chickpea flour

1 tsp ginger-garlic paste

½ tsp kashmiri red chilli powder / lal mirch powder

½ tsp turmeric / haldi

1 green chilli

½ tsp coriander powder

½ tsp cumin powder
